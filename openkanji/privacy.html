Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026

OpenKanji ("the app") is a free, offline-first Android application for learning Japanese kanji. This policy explains what data the app does and does not handle.

Summary

  • No account is required. OpenKanji has no login and does not ask for your name, email, or any personal identifier.
  • Your learning data stays on your device. Your study progress, review schedule, and settings are stored locally and are never uploaded to us.
  • No analytics or tracking SDKs are included by the developer.
  • The app works fully offline for its core features.

Data the app stores on your device

The following are stored locally only and never transmitted to the developer:

  • Spaced-repetition review state (which kanji/words you've studied and when they are next due).
  • App settings (theme, font, handwriting strictness, reminder preference).

You can erase all of this at any time by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app.

Handwriting recognition

Handwriting practice and "draw to search" use Google ML Kit Digital Ink Recognition, which runs on your device. The first time you use it, the app downloads a Japanese recognition model over the internet. Your drawings are processed locally and are not sent to the developer. Google's handling of ML Kit is governed by Google's own terms and privacy policy.

Advertising

This section applies only if/when ads are enabled in a released version.

If the app displays ads, it uses Google AdMob. AdMob may collect and use certain data (such as device identifiers) to serve and measure ads, as described in Google's Privacy Policy. Where required, the app presents a consent prompt (Google User Messaging Platform) so you can control personalized advertising. Ads, if present, are shown sparingly and never during study or practice.

Network access

The app connects to the internet only to download the on-device handwriting recognition model (one time), and — if ads are enabled — to request and display ads and show the consent form. No other data leaves your device.

Children's privacy

OpenKanji does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new "last updated" date.
